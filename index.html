Covid-19

stay safe, stay home

Coronaviruses (CoV) are a large family of viruses that cause illness ranging from the common cold to more severe diseases such as Middle East Respiratory Syndrome (MERS-CoV) and Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome (SARS-CoV). A novel coronavirus (nCoV) is a new strain that has not been previously identified in humans.

Wear A Face Mask

Wash Your Hands

Wash your hands often with soap and water for at least 20 seconds especially after you have been in a public place, or after blowing your nose, coughing, or sneezing Before eating or preparing food Before touching your face After using the restroom After leaving a public place After blowing your nose, coughing, or sneezing After handling your mask After changing a diaper After caring for someone sick After touching animals or pets

Avoid Close Contact

Being in crowds like in restaurants, bars, fitness centers, or movie theaters puts you at higher risk for COVID-19. Avoid indoor spaces that do not offer fresh air from the outdoors as much as possible. If indoors, bring in fresh air by opening windows and doors, if possible.

What Are The Main Symptoms?

COVID-19 affects different people in different ways. Most infected people will develop mild to moderate illness and recover without hospitalization.

  • fever
  • Tiredness
  • Dry Cough
  • Sore Throat
  • Aches and Pains
  • Shortness of Breath

things not to do during covid

Stay home. Most people with COVID-19 have mild illness and can recover at home without medical care. Do not leave your home, except to get medical care. Do not visit public areas.

  • Do Not Share Eating
  • Do Not Touch Your Face or Nose
  • Do Not Contact Sick People

things to do during covid

Take care of yourself. Get rest and stay hydrated. Take over-the-counter medicines, such as acetaminophen, to help you feel better.
